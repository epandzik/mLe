HubSpot Email Hack

08.03.2015

This came up earlier last month, but I thought I should share the solution. There are a variety of sites that provide all-in-one marketing software. It brings together various SEO, social media and blogging strategies. It lets you tailor your website for generating leads and create workflows that will convert those leads to sales.

I work with HubSpot at my job to maintain their leads and workflows. One problem with this site is that it lets the user create email templates that should follow their own design and branding. Now — this isn’t the problem. The real problem is Outlook. And it’s lack of many styling cues present in most other email clients.

I was put on a project for my company trying to make an email that would be part of our new product roll-out. So on a fine Tuesday morning, I jumped into my first HubSpot experience. I started using their Template Builder since I figured that it would be the smoothest experience with their software. Mostly that’s correct. Set it up, looks great in their preview and in most email clients. The issue came in when I used their Litmus test to preview the email in various Outlook versions. As you can see, the layout gets pretty jagged from what I wanted to see.

So I called customer support (a few times) and get various suggestions for my layout issues. Make both rounds of changes, one at a time and — hrm, a different spacing problem.

Next morning I’m much less frustrated, back at work with a higher level of sleep and well-caffeinated. I call in again and am referred to the various FAQs (Why do emails look different in Outlook?, Why is the spacing different in Outlook vs other email clients? and Why are margins and padding around my image not rendering in Outlook?) describing how HubSpot can’t control how Outlook renders its emails. Now, having already had this conversation with my boss, I know that that "it can't" is not an acceptable answer.

So I go through the FAQs and find that there is one link on one of the pages that refers me to Various Styling on Web Client. I find a lovely table that confirms, yes — Outlook destroys styling. Basically I can use tables and margins. That’s it.

I go back to the drawing board (rather the Template Builder on HubSpot) and make each of the modules into tables rather than inserting the image into the text and styling the text separately. Again, the Preview looks great. So I send it through Litmus and have an even more macabre pile of versions in Outlook.

At this point, I’m just about ready to slap my computer and call it names. But I take a tour of the office and think about anything but this for a few minutes. Blanking out my brains sometimes allows ideas to float up, and this was one of those times.

I get rid of their many modules and make one table in one module. This actually works! A bit more styling (and editing) also fixes my mobile issue.
